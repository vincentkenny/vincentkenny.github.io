What to do?

This is a visualizer of a K-Means clustering algorithm. The scattered data will be clustered based on the centroids.
Start by determining the number of available groups, and then scattering the data. The options are beside each other.
The scattered data will also consist of the centroids representing the first seeds of the groups. The centroid will move each cycle of the visualization relative to the positions of the group member. Nodes takes a color as identity according to the closest centroid to its position.
If you want to add more data, you can drag over or click at the grid. The initial centroids cannot be replaced.
You can stop the visualization process with clearing the board.
Whenever you're ready, start the algorithm by pressing the most obvious button. Have fun!
